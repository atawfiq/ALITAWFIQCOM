Keys & Arrival

The door locks automatically. Never step out without your keys — there's no override. Seriously, tattoo this onto your hand if needed.

Your set of keys

We left a spare set hanging on the peg board inside — it's yours for the duration of the stay.

Windows

No screens on the windows — please only open them slightly, as it's genuinely dangerous for Dust. Even a small gap can be enough for her to escape. Close all windows when you head out.

Cameras

We have three cameras in the apartment — we turn them on when we're away to check on things. For your privacy, please unplug the bedroom camera when you settle in. You'll find it easily. Plug it back in before you leave. The other two are just keeping an eye on the common spaces (and a certain cat).

Dust, Our Cat

The real host of this apartment

Meet Dust.

She's a little weird, occasionally dramatic, and will absolutely judge you — but give her time and she'll be sleeping next to you within the day. The more you ignore her, the more she'll want your attention. Classic.

On arrival: We leave Dust in the den with the door closed while we're away. When you get home, please let her out and leave the den door open so she can roam freely. She doesn't tend to get on furniture — she'll occasionally sit on the grey chair by the window — and won't get on the bed, though she does love to hide underneath it. If she's being too active at night, feel free to put her back in the den and shut the door. She's completely comfortable there and actually likes it.
Windows & the front door: Please don't let Dust outside — she's an indoor cat. Be careful when opening the front door that she doesn't make a run for it. And remember: no window screens — even a small opening is dangerous for her. Only crack them slightly and never leave windows open when you go out.

If she hisses or swats — she doesn't hate you. She's just theatrical. It passes.

All her things

Everything Dust needs is in the cupboard below the air fryer: her treats (called Churu), food refills (in the big clear container), litter bags, litter deodorizer, wipes. Wood pellet litter refill is in the coat closet, below the vacuum. Lint rollers are on the counter — for you.
